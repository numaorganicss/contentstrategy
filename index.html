Voice & Tone

How a Numa post should sound out loud.

Read every caption aloud before publishing. If it sounds like a shouting marketer, rewrite it. If it sounds like a wise older sister whispering across the dinner table, you're home.

Do this

  • Speak softly and with reverence — short sentences, lots of breath.
  • Use lowercase intentionally for poetry, capital letters for product education.
  • Quote scripture sparingly and beautifully, never as a sales tactic.
  • Name ingredients by their origin (e.g. "shea from the Northern Region").
  • Use sensory language — texture, warmth, scent, slowness.
  • Invite reflection: "what does your skin remember?"
  • Centre the customer's becoming, not the product's features.
  • Use Ghanaian Pidgin or Twi naturally where it fits ("chale", "small small").
  • Show real hands, real skin tones, real textures.
  • End with a soft CTA: "save this for Sunday", "tag someone you love".

Don't do this

  • Don't shout in all caps or use aggressive sales language.
  • Don't promise dermatological miracles or use "cure", "treat", "heal" loosely.
  • Don't use scripture out of context to pressure a sale.
  • Don't shame skin types, body types, or skincare neglect harshly.
  • Don't trash other brands or compare aggressively.
  • Don't post stock photography that doesn't match Ghanaian skin.
  • Don't over-edit or over-filter our products until they look fake.
  • Don't use heavy emojis or trending slang that ages our voice.
  • Don't ignore questions in DMs — every reply is brand-building.
  • Don't post on autopilot during sensitive cultural moments without checking.
